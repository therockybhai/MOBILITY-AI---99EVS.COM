Inspection Cycles — By Segment

Inspection requirements and component analysis differ dramatically across vehicle segments. Select your segment.

🛵 Two-Wheeler — Complete Parts Analysis
🔌 Engine / Motor
Petrol: oil level, oil quality, engine noise, smoke. EV: motor temperature, winding insulation, bearing wear.
🔴 Braking System
Disc brake: pad thickness >2mm, rotor flatness. Drum: shoe clearance. CBS/ABS: functional self-test on start.
🔋 Battery
12V: terminal corrosion, voltage 12.4-12.7V. EV main: SoH indicator, cell temperature, BMS status light.
🔄 Tyres
Pressure: 28-32 PSI front, 30-36 PSI rear (varies by model). Tread depth: >1.5mm. Age: replace by 5 years.
⚽ Chain / Belt
Chain: 2-3% slack, lubricated. Belt drive: no fraying, tension correct. Shaft drive: gear oil level.
💡 Electrical
All lights: headlight, indicators, taillight, horn. Switches: clean and dry. Wiring: no exposed wire near hot parts.
🔨 Suspension
Front forks: oil seals (no leaks), travel smooth. Rear: monoshock/twin absorbers — bounce test (should rebound once).
📜 Controls
Throttle: smooth return, no sticking. Clutch: biting point, free play 2-3mm. Brake lever/pedal: firm feel.

🚗 Car / SUV — Complete Parts Analysis
⚙️ Engine
Oil level (cold), coolant level, belt condition (serpentine/timing), air filter, spark plugs (petrol), glow plugs (diesel).
🔴 Brakes (4-wheel)
Front disc: pad thickness, rotor condition. Rear disc/drum: inspection. Brake fluid: level + colour (dark = replace). ABS warning light.
🔄 Tyres (5 including spare)
Pressure: 28-36 PSI (door placard). Tread: >2mm. Rotation: every 10,000 km. Alignment: every 20,000 km. Spare: inflated and accessible.
💡 Lights & Electrical
All 4 indicators, headlights (high/low), brake lights, reversing light, hazard lights, horn. OBD-II: no fault codes.
🌫️ HVAC / AC
Cabin filter: annual. AC refrigerant: every 2 years. Heater core: flush if coolant contaminated.
🔨 Suspension
Struts/shocks: bounce test. Ball joints, tie rods: check for play. Wheel bearings: listen for rumble on turns.
👥 Safety Systems
Airbags: no warning light. Seat belts: retract fully, pretensioner intact. Child lock: test rear doors.
📷 Documents
RC, Insurance, PUC certificate, Driving licence. DigiLocker for digital copies. Renewal tracking in calendar.

⚡ EV-Specific Inspection — Battery to Motor
🔋 High-Voltage Battery Pack
Cell voltage variance (<50mV imbalance is healthy). State of Health (SoH): >80% acceptable. Temperature: 15–35°C optimal. Visible damage or deformation: immediate stop.
🧪 BMS (Battery Management System)
Error codes: check via OBD/app. Cell balancing: active during charge. Thermal runaway detection: working. SOC accuracy: within 3% after full cycle.
📅 Charging System
Onboard charger: no error light. CCS2/Type-2/Bharat DC connector: pins clean, no corrosion. Charge rate: consistent with rated kW.
⛬ Traction Motor
Temperature: <120°C under normal load. Noise: no grinding/rattling. Power output: consistent with rated torque. Winding insulation resistance: >1 MΩ.
🔌 Regenerative Braking
Regen level: selectable in most EVs. Brake blending: smooth, no harsh engagement. Regen contribution: reduces mechanical brake wear 40-60%.
🌫️ Thermal Management
Battery coolant: level and clarity (change every 4 years). Cooling pump: operating pressure normal. Battery temperature delta: <5°C across cells is healthy.

Driving & Riding Basics — Safe Start to Safe Stop
🔋 Before You Start — Pre-Ride / Pre-Drive Check
  • Documents: Licence, RC, Insurance, PUC in DigiLocker or physical. Penalty for missing: ₹500–₹5,000.
  • PPE: Helmet (IS-4151) for 2W — mandatory. Seatbelt for car drivers/all passengers. Gloves and jacket for 2W long rides.
  • Quick checks: Tyre pressure (takes 30 seconds). Fuel/charge level. All lights working. Brakes responsive. Mirrors aligned.
  • Mental state: Never ride/drive when tired, unwell, or after alcohol. BAC limit: 30 mg/100 ml blood.
🔄 Starting Off — Basics Every Rider/Driver Must Know
  • Two-wheelers: Both feet down when stopped. Indicate before moving. Look over shoulder before pulling out. Clutch fully in before gear change.
  • Cars: Mirrors (MSPSL — Mirror, Signal, Position, Speed, Look) before every manoeuvre. Hill starts: handbrake + clutch control. Smooth acceleration — no jerks.
  • EVs: Fully silent at low speed — be especially aware of pedestrians. Regenerative braking: lift throttle early for gradual deceleration.
  • Commercial: Blind spots are larger — check mirrors before every lane change. Wide turns on roundabouts.
🚨 Traffic Rules — India (MV Act 2019)
  • Speed limits: City 30-50 km/h · State highway 60-80 km/h · National highway 80-100 km/h · Expressway 100-120 km/h.
  • Lane discipline: Keep left (India drives left). Overtaking: right side only. No straddling lane markings.
  • Intersections: Traffic signal: stop at stop line. Yellow = slow, not speed up. Right of way: vehicle on the right at equal intersections.
  • Pedestrians: Absolute right of way at zebra crossings. Stop — do not honk at crossings. School zones: max 25 km/h.
  • Indicators: Signal 30 metres before turn. Cancel after completing. Hazard lights: only when genuinely hazardous.
  • Honking: Prohibited near hospitals, schools, courts (silence zones). Use sparingly — one short tap sufficient. No continuous horn.
📄 Challan Management & Compliance
  • Check pending challans: Parivahan.gov.in or mParivahan app → Vehicle RC number → View all challans. Pay online within 30 days to avoid licence suspension.
  • Common challans: No helmet ₹1,000 · No seatbelt ₹1,000 · Red light jump ₹5,000 + 6-month suspension · No insurance ₹2,000 (first) / ₹4,000 (repeat) · Drunk driving ₹10,000 + imprisonment.
  • PUC certificate: Get done at any authorised PUC centre. Petrol/CNG/Diesel: every 6 months to 1 year per state. Carry physical or DigiLocker copy.
  • Fitness certificate: Vehicles older than 15 years require annual fitness from VAHAN-authorised centre. Failure: registration cancellation.

Safety Notice
Important information about how MobilityAI should be used

MobilityAI provides educational information about mobility systems, vehicle technologies, and safety practices.

Scope of Information

The information on this platform is designed to help users understand how vehicles and mobility systems work. It is based on engineering principles, manufacturer documentation, and publicly available technical information.

What This Platform Does Not Replace

  • Professional mechanical inspection by a qualified technician
  • Certified engineering advice for structural or safety-critical repairs
  • Manufacturer service guidelines specific to your vehicle
  • Authorised dealer or service centre assessment
  • Emergency services in active vehicle emergency situations

Your Responsibility

Always follow official manufacturer recommendations for your specific vehicle. Vehicle systems are complex and vary by make, model, year, and condition. Information provided here is general and educational.

For active vehicle emergencies — fire, brake failure while moving, loss of steering, sudden tyre burst — contact emergency services immediately.

Professional Referral

MobilityAI will always recommend consulting a qualified technician for any repair or inspection that involves safety-critical systems including brakes, steering, suspension, fuel systems, and high-voltage EV components.

Responsible AI Principles
How MobilityAI is designed to serve people safely and ethically

MobilityAI is designed to support human learning and safety in mobility systems. The platform follows these core principles in all interactions.

Human Safety Above All

Safety overrides every other consideration. If a query indicates an active dangerous situation, the platform responds with immediate safety guidance before any knowledge delivery. Human life is the absolute priority.

Information Without Commercial Bias

Information is provided neutrally without commercial bias. No brand partnerships, advertising, or commercial arrangements influence the content or recommendations made by MobilityAI.

Dangerous Practices Are Never Encouraged

Unsafe modifications, illegal vehicle alterations, dangerous repair procedures, tampering with safety systems, and any practice that violates manufacturer guidelines are explicitly refused by the AI reasoning system.

Education, Not Replacement of Expertise

MobilityAI aims to empower people with understanding, not replace professional expertise. For all safety-critical repairs and inspections, the platform recommends consulting qualified professionals.

Transparency in AI Reasoning

Every response passes through a 5-layer reasoning process: domain identification, vehicle system analysis, global context adaptation, safety evaluation, and structured knowledge delivery. This structure is applied consistently.

Mobility Knowledge Is Shared for Responsible Use

Knowledge is provided for education, prevention, and responsible mobility decisions. The platform does not encourage reckless behaviour, illegal modifications, or practices that endanger users or others.

Knowledge Governance Framework
How MobilityAI ensures accuracy, neutrality, and safety in all knowledge

MobilityAI maintains a structured knowledge governance system to ensure that all mobility information is accurate, neutral, and safety-focused.

Knowledge Sources

Information is derived from engineering principles, manufacturer documentation, mobility research publications, transport safety reports, and standards bodies including BIS, AIS, CMVR, ISO, and SAE. Unverified or anecdotal information is not published.

Layered Reasoning Architecture

Every response follows a 5-layer reasoning approach that evaluates:

  • Layer 1 — Domain Identification: Classifies the query into the correct mobility domain
  • Layer 2 — Vehicle System Analysis: Identifies systems and components involved
  • Layer 3 — Context Adaptation: Adjusts for regional regulations, climate, and infrastructure
  • Layer 4 — Safety Evaluation: Evaluates all safety risks before response delivery
  • Layer 5 — Structured Education: Delivers knowledge in a structured, clear format

Knowledge Update Pipeline

New topics enter a controlled 5-stage pipeline before publication: Insight Detected → Knowledge Candidate → Validation Review → Structured Topic → Published. This prevents uncontrolled or inaccurate knowledge expansion.

Validation Criteria

Every topic is validated for: technical accuracy, clear engineering explanation, safety compliance, neutral and unbiased presentation, and credible source citation where applicable.

Structured Format

All substantive topics follow a 9-section structure: Overview, Engineering Principle, Key Components, Operation Logic, Calculations, Safety Notes, Maintenance, Diagnostics, and Research Insights. This ensures consistent quality across all knowledge domains.
